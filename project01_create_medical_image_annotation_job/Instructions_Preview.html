Pneumonia Classification

In this job, you will be presented with chest X-ray images. Your task is to review each image and determine if there are signs of pneumonia present.

In this example, you can see clear lungs without any abnormal cloudiness or opacity. The web-like vasculature is visible, and there is a clear diaphragm shadow below the lungs. This image would be classified as "No" for the presence of pneumonia.

A pneumonia image may include a few things: areas of cloudiness/opacity in several concentrated areas or one large area. You may also see a general pattern of opacity that obscures the lungs, heart, and diaphragm structure. Below are examples of pneumonia symptoms: (Left) a concentrated, opaque area in the lungs, (Right) multiple, smaller opaque areas throughout the lung area, and any diaphragm shadow is obscured.

Steps

  1. Carefully review the provided chest X-ray image.
  2. If the image appears to be a normal, healthy chest X-ray without any signs of pneumonia, select No and move on to the next image.
  3. If you identify clear signs of pneumonia in the image, such as areas of cloudiness, opacity, or a general pattern obscuring the lungs, heart, and diaphragm structure, select Yes. Then, proceed to answer the follow-up question by checking the appropriate symptom(s) observed in the image before moving on to the next image.
  4. If you are uncertain about the presence of pneumonia in the image due to poor image quality, ambiguous findings, or any other reason, select Not Sure. For the follow-up question, indicate your level of confidence in the likelihood of pneumonia being present in the image. After answering, continue to the next image.

Rules:

  • When determining the presence of pneumonia in a chest X-ray image, focus solely on identifying areas of opaqueness and assessing the visibility of the diaphragm shadow, as described in the overview section.
  • If multiple pneumonia symptoms are present in a single image, you have the option to select all applicable checkboxes in the symptoms question.
  • When selecting the Not Sure option, use the provided rating scale to indicate your level of confidence in the likelihood of pneumonia being present. A rating of 1 represents Not at all likely, while a rating of 5 signifies Extremely likely.

Tips:

  • Pay close attention to the images, as pneumonia may manifest as a single concentrated, opaque area in the lungs or as multiple smaller opaque areas distributed throughout the lung region. In some cases, the diaphragm shadow may be obscured due to the presence of pneumonia.
  • Take your time to carefully examine each image, but try to maintain a steady pace to ensure efficient completion of the annotation task.
  • If you encounter any ambiguous or challenging images, don't hesitate to use the Not Sure option and provide your best assessment of the likelihood of pneumonia using the rating scale.
  • Regularly refer to the provided examples of healthy and pneumonia-affected chest X-rays to refresh your understanding of the key visual indicators.
